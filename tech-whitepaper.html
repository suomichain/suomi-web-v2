Abstract

Credit plays a critical role in all business activities. For example, in the financial industry, all financial regulatory activities and intermediaries, including product registration, securities issuance and trading, information disclosure and fund custody, are solutions to resolve the trust or asymmetric information issues. Another example is the supply chain management, which consists of suppliers, manufacturers, distributors, retailers, consumers, etc. To achieve the perfect coordination, the whole network needs trust. As a social problem that has always been difficult to solve, the current trust issue in commercial activities could be solved precisely by the blockchain technology at lower cost.

As an infrastructure of the blockchain platform, which fully utilizes the characteristics of blockchain, such as decentralization, autonomy and unmodified information, SUOMI aims at further integrating the blockchain technology with business and social activities, and establishing a comprehensive fundamental for blockchain service platform. The applications of SUOMI are very broad. For instance, in the area of supply chain finance, the blockchain makes the data more open and transparent, which help the relevant enterprises and financial institutions to evaluate the assets and operation risks more accurately and to provide more customized support for business matching and fraud monitoring. To streamline the implementation of the consortium blockchain, SUOMI provides integration capabilities to customize the transaction rules and consensus algorithm by on-chain governance.

In addition, SUOMI will combine blockchain with edge intelligence technology to construct an infrastructure of Internet of Things, such as a distributed device network, which ran by a lot of equipments autonomously. The advantage of these technology combinations is that IoT devices could process large amounts of data generated by human or inhuman activities, which not only help enterprises solve the huge burden of cloud data operations in the Internet of Things era, but also make the devices themselves intelligent, known as artificial Intelligent Decentralized Autonomous Organization (AI DAO). The edge intelligence can adapt its own role in organizational activities through effective and timely processing of complex information and data to build a stable, secure, reliable and autonomous ecosystem.

Design Concept Blockchain Almost all mechanisms in human society are centralized. Even after the Internet revolution, a large number of social activities were digitized, the centralized and trustworthy third parties dominated most of the peer-to-peer value delivery scenarios. The emerging of the blockchain has made it possible for both parties without a trust base to collaborate and complete the transfer of value without the assistance of a third party and is therefore considered as a potential solution to replace the current centralized system of the whole society. Blockchain originated in the underlying Bitcoin technology, which consists of a variety of technologies such as peer-to-peer network, distributed data storage, consensus mechanism, encryption algorithm and so on. As a distrusted and decentralized distributed ledger technology, blockchain has become a popular research topic in many industries in the past few years. Design Backgroundï¼ Centralized systems dominate the public domain because of the requirements of high efficiency, which is also its competitive advantage compared with the corresponding decentralized systems. It can be said that with the maturity of the current blockchain technology, there is no sufficient capability for decentralized system to overtake the centralized system in most of the public service. Therefore, SUOMI believes that the blockchain, as the trust machine, will have more potential applications in non-public domain (such as enterprise alliances) public domain where the centralized system has fatal flaws (such as charities, lack of open and transparent information) new areas, e.g., unprofitable scenarios for middle-man or immature intermediaries such as IoT, artificial intelligence. Design Goalï¼Build a Global Blockchain Ecosystem The design goal of SUOMI is to become the fundamental for building different types of blockchain in various scenarios, that is, to construct the underlying protocol of the blockchain in a SUOMI manner, which solves the security, scalability and privacy problems of the blockchain, and provides as many modules and plugins as possible in the application layer, and support popular high-level languages and tools to help individual developers or enterprises build, deploy, and run their smart contracts rapidly according to their own business needs. Architecture and Technology The design goal of SUOMI is to implement smart contracts that meet the needs of complex social activities through a distributed ledger and share the data in a highly customizable manner without mutual trust. In addition, SUOMI has the following characteristics: By separating the application layer and the core system, SUOMI simplify the development of platform applications Give developers more options to choose the right trading rules, forms of authorization, and consensus algorithms when creating a SuoDApp based on their business needs For the non-dependent transactions, the platform supports parallel transactions to improve block creation and verification speed Integrate state-of-the-art software architecture, consensus algorithms and trusted execution environment Core Architecture There are five key components for SUOMI architecture: A peer-to-peer networkï¼Transfer messages between nodes Distributed Ledger: Store the list of ordered transactions Consensus Algorithm: Ensure the consistency and integrity of ordered transactions and resulting states Smart Contract Logic: Transaction execution Distributed Storage: Store the resulting states SUOMI adopts the âtraditionalâ blockchain architecture as its core layer, including gossip protocol and a linked chain of cryptographically transaction blocks. Furthermore, SUOMI designs a modular consensus interface and a transaction processing engine to handle the different scenarios, threats and smart contracts. Peer-to-Peer Network The nodes in SUOMI pass the message over the TCP protocol in the network. The contents of the message include transactions, blocks, and other nodes in the network. These structured messages are all serialized using Google Protocol Buffers. By utilizing the gossip protocol, SUOMI not only decouples the network structure, separates the transaction order and transaction processing to optimize network performance and scalability, but also propagates transactions and blocks to all nodes in the network at the same time, ensuring the consistency and integrity of transaction data. This design is consistent with most public chains such as Ethereum and Bitcoin. In order to meet the needs of enterprise-grade deployment, SUOMI supports network authentication at the P2P layer. By controlling the access list, SUOMI node can control: Whether the node is allowed to connect to the network and can keep the current account status Whether the node allows sending the consensus information and participation in the consensus process. Whether the node can submit the transaction record in the network Distributed Ledger One of the most important features of blockchain and state machine approach is the ability to ensure that the network is consistent in its order. SUOMI, like traditional blockchain techniques, organizes the transaction logically into the "blockchain" data structure that contains an ordered list of encrypted blocks whose hash pointers point to the genesis block. Each block in the list contains an ordered list of transactions. The nodes agree on the order of transactions recorded in the ledger by the consensus algorithm. SUOMI does not require that each transaction be in strict order (that is, each transaction is before or after the other), and the transaction scheduler will allow transactions without dependencies to be executed parallelly. It also allows SUOMI to improve scalability and reduce storage requirements by partition and sharding. Smart Contract Smart contracts can be viewed as a state machine, which means the transaction from the distributed ledger could be input to the current snapshot of the state, and produce an output to update the state in the distributed ledger. This shows that the blockchain and the traditional state machine replication architecture is very similar. Blockchain uses this layer to implement business logic and domain capabilities for specific use cases. Because of its high security and scalability requirements, Bitcoin offers only very limited scripting languages ââon smart contracts, and its blockchain is stateless, meaning there is no data storage in processing and canât run multi-phase execution. If the operation of the smart contract succeeds, the input is consumed and a new unspent output is created while if the operation fails, there is no change to the blockchain. This design addresses the non-functional needs of Bitcoin, but also makes some very simple contract rules infeasible. Ethereum extends the concept of smart contracts to deal with transactions on virtual machines. Smart contracts are written in high-level languages, such as Solidity, and compiled into binary code that can run on the Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM). The logic of smart contract can be dynamically deployed across the blockchain via contract-wide broadcast. The operation code of contract can be used by network peers to verify transactions. Once the transaction is processed, the internal state of the smart contract is written to decentralized storage. SUOMI extends the concept of smart contracts to state machines, the "SUOMI Transaction Processing Engineâ, also known as STPE. After inputting a series of distributed ledgers, the transaction will find the correct STPE to enter the valid transaction data along with the relevant pre-transaction status. SUOMI is also a model that supports both stateless UTXO (such as Bitcoin) and stateful (such as Ethereum). SUOMI allows developers to develop their own smart contracts in many popular languages, including: Interpreted language: Python, JavaScript Compiled language: C + +, Go Virtual Machine: EVM, Java By using these well-established programming languages, you can take advantage of existing tools to make static, dynamic, and formal analysis of contract codes. Distributed Storage Another important task that most blockchain applications need to address is how to ensure that states are consistent across the entire network, which is also an important goal of state machine replication. In Bitcoinâs blockchain, the status is a list of bitcoins that has not yet been spent, whereas the status managed by Ethereum includes the internal status of the balance of the native currency and the smart contract running on its network. SUOMI uses a method similar to Ethereum to store serialized contract states using radix Merkel tree. Each STPE is assigned to a namespace with write permission and can be serialized in a different way. Consensus Algorithm Consensus is a process that allows network nodes to agree on the order and outcome of the transaction. Most consensus algorithms fall into two categories: Lottery system: This is a mechanism that select a leader with accounting rights. The leader proposes a new block and inform other nodes acknowledge it. Compared with the voting system, the scalability of this method is good, which can be used in a large-scale network while the disadvantage is that this algorithm is highly likely make the situation that two leaders proposed different blocks. It takes a longer time to solve the forks. The typical lottery-based algorithms are PoW, PoS and DPoS. Voting: This system requires each node to transfer information to other nodes for multiple rounds of voting, so the more nodes in the network, the longer it takes to reach a consensus. Compared with lottery system, voting has the advantage of finality, which means much easier to reach a final agreement. The core design principle of the algorithm is how to balance scalability and efficiency. The typical examples of this category are Practical Byzantine Fault Tolerance (for malicious nodes) and Raft (for no malicious nodes). Because each consensus algorithm has its own unique applications and properties (such as network size, throughput, fault tolerance), SUOMI only provides consensus algorithm interface and gives the developers the option to choose the appropriate consensus algorithm. SUOMI has implemented or planned to provide the following consensus algorithms: Definition Implementation Application Random Leader A simple lottery-based consensus algorithm Developer mode FastPoW A response time based consensus algorithm Any environments (no BFT) Trust-based FastPoW FastPoW in trusted execution environment hardware-assisted implementation, for larger networks PBFT To reach consensus via multiple rounds votes for smaller network such as consortium blockchain Raft Vote for a leader to publish the new block Any environments (no BFT) Technologies By summarizing the application scenarios, SUOMI is designed to be a highly modular, high throughput and high security framework with a variety of unique mechanisms, so that developers and enterprises can easily target their business requirements on the platform to build their applications. Simplify the Application Development and Deployment The architecture of SUOMI divides the system's core layer and the smart contract layer into clear boundaries, which are abstracted through smart contracts, allowing developers to write SuoDApps in many popular languages. SUOMI Smart Contract Development Platform (SCDP) supports a growing number of popular languages, including Java, Python, JavaScript, Go, C++, and provides a set of trading libraries containing a large number of underlying functions as a template for developer to use. The development platform also supports JSON as a standard data format and uses the REST API to interact with the client. Permissioning The developers can deploy private chains based on their private keys. Transaction information in private chain is completely independent. The administrators of private chain have member management interface that allow them to determine the access level of information in the private chain through roles and authentication. Parallel Transaction Execution A typical single-threaded blockchain requires each transaction execute in an order. SUOMI expanded its mechanism based on the single-thread model. The transaction scheduler will split non-dependent transactions into different processing streams for parallel execution. With the prerequisite to prevent the double-spent issues, several transactions were executed at the same time to accelerate the creation and verification of the blocks on chain. Distributed Governance SUOMI allows the on-chain change management through a specific transaction library "On-chain Configuration", which covers the decisions like whether to change the consensus algorithm or change the transaction engine (STPE). "On-chain Configuration" supports two different modes: Administrator mode: the system administrator can modify the settings of blockchain after authorization and authentication directly. Community mode: community members vote for a policy. When most of nodes agree on the new policy, the changes blockchain configuration will be executed. Key Technologies The key technologies of SUOMI include FastPoW, highly expressive and safe smart contract, and SUOMI Transaction Processing Engine (STPE). Fast Proof of Work (FastPoWï¼ According to the design goal of SUOMI, FastPoW consensus algorithm can handle more than 1000 transactions per second, the workflow is shown as follows (the flow chart is on the next page): First, candidates that meet the election eligibility in the network are selected through a Verifiable Random Generator (VRG) Then, each candidate node gets a randomly elected time from the VRG The candidate nodes with the shortest time of random elected time will be named the leader of this round, who also have the rights to publish the new block to the ledgers, and obtain the corresponding token reward. After completing the accounting, the leader node requests a random waiting time through the random number generator, and the node must spend a random waiting time to obtain the candidate again to avoid taking the advantage of successive publishing blocks. FastPoW offers a fair leadership election through VRG, avoiding heavy energy-consuming work competition among a large number of nodes. FastPoW which develops the Nakamoto's POW algorithm in an eco-friendly manner, includes several key features: Fair and reliable: All participants should have equal opportunities for participation Reasonable return on investment: The cost of the leader election process should be much lower than the value the leader can generate Simple verification: Each participant can easily verify whether leaders are legally elected. Verified Random Function Generator Since most of the blockchain consensus algorithms are using random numbers to solve the randomness issue, obtaining a random number that is difficult to predict and control is the core of the validity of consensus algorithm. After extensive research, the traditional random number generation issues have been solved well, but it does not completely solve the problem to let participants verify the result of random number generation in decentralized environment. SUOMI verifiable random number generator (VRG) constructs a generic dice based on the Verifiable Random Functions (VRF). Suomi uses the random numbers generated by VRG multiple times to determine the results of election and the waiting time for the restricted nodes. The simple introduction of VRF describes as follows: There is a function F, the node can use its own private key pk and want to deliver the information x, as a random number input, and the output is random number r and proof of x. Other nodes can verify that the random number r that comes from F by using the ppkâs corresponding public key pbk and the proof of x. The difference between a digital signature and VRF is that VRF has one and only one output. There are many ways to construct the function of VRF. Currently, SUOMI uses verifiable random numbers based on polynomial interpolation . By using blocks to construct F to simplify calculation, the construction process is briefly described as follows: Each node constructs a polynomial based on the hash values of the nearest n blocks so that the polynomial curve can pass through each hash pair to obtain polynomial coefficients such that each node generates a random number r and proof according to the polynomial . If a node suspects the result of a random number generated by another node, it is only needs to substitute r and proof into the function A (x) to verify the results. Applications Blockchain and next-generation information technologies such as cloud computing, big data, the Internet of things, 5G, artificial intelligence, have full potential to integrate together to create a new economy area. The application scenarios are very broad. Supply Chain Finance Supply chain finance refers to financing various parties in the supply chain through different financing instruments, which balance the liquidity gap between buyers and sellers and minimize the total costs of supply chain. Because of the large number of participants in the supply chain, including raw materials suppliers, manufacturers, distributors, retailers. The complex network structure consists of logistics, information and capital flow. Additionally, the data and information in the supply chain operation are generated from different sources. The information is invisible or unclear. For example, banks canât see the original contract between enterprises and verify the use of funds. This kind of issues make the banks only provide prepayment or inventory financing for first level suppliers. Therefore, a large number of second or third level suppliers/ distributors are very difficult to obtain funds from the traditional financial channels and push them to higher interest funds through other channels, which limits the development speed of small and medium-size enterprises. It also limits the areas of supply chain financial services. By utilizing SUOMI platform, the alliance between enterprises and financial institutions can set effective business rules and eliminate illegal and malicious operations on the blockchain based on the transaction libraries, such as digitizing warehouse receipts, contracts and bills on the supply chain and regulating the transfer. After that, digitized assets can play a self-evident role in the whole process, which reduces a lot of manpower and resources to test the authenticity of the transaction and the cost of risk control. Therefore, the transaction speed and cost will be much lower than the traditional centralized banking system. Peer-to-Peer Device Network As an extended network based on the Internet, the Internet of Things (IoT) relied on a centralized server model historically. This model can no longer meet the growing needs in the near future due to the high cost. The trends of decentralized IoT pattern is imperative. Through peer-to-peer networking technology, IoT deals with the communication between different devices and distributes the computing and storage needs from the original centralized server to a large number of equipments, which coordinates through blockchain, to cut costs significantly. Charities In the past few years, social charities and lotteries have been frequently questioned by the public about the usage of funds. As an industry that relies on information transparency for its credibility, the current mode of social welfare requires an urgent transformation. Based on the distributed ledger technology, blockchain enables each donor to fully understand the distribution of the fund, which can be an excellent tool to improve the public trust. It is also applicable to complex business scenarios such as directed donation and conditional donation. SUOMI hands over the choice of consensus algorithms to developers, providing only algorithmic implementation and pluggable interfaces to meet the needs of the different types of blockchains. Through the accumulation of the industry practice, SUOMI formed a huge knowledge base and made the smart contract fulfil the complex needs of different industries without sacrificing security.
